# Persona Template

Use this file to define a new audience for ShipSignal. Copy it, rename it
(e.g. `enterprise-admin.md`), and fill it in.

The goal is not to describe a demographic. The goal is to make the person so
real that anyone on your team could write to them without ever meeting them.
A great persona makes you feel like you know someone specific.

---

## Before You Write: Answer These First

These questions are harder than they look. Sit with them.

**1. Who is the last person who contacted you about this product?**
What were they actually trying to do? What frustrated them? What did they say
when they were being honest, not polite?

**2. If your product disappeared tomorrow, what would this person lose?**
Not features — what would they lose in their actual day, their actual job,
their actual life?

**3. What is this person responsible for that scares them?**
Everyone has something that could go wrong on their watch. What's theirs?

**4. What does this person do in the five minutes before they read your release note?**
Where are they? What device? What were they just doing? This shapes everything
about how you talk to them.

**5. What's the one question they're trying to answer?**
Not "what changed" — what are they actually trying to figure out?
("Is this safe to approve?" / "Will my workflow break?" / "Should I tell my boss?")

## Persona File Structure

Fill in each section below. The more specific and concrete, the better.
Avoid adjectives without evidence. "Busy" tells you nothing. "Three direct reports,
14 active accounts, and a weekly board update" tells you everything.
